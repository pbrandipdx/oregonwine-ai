# Crushpad.ai
## Wine Agent
# Match Me

## Tagline
A 4-question personality quiz that matches users to a winery.

BuzzFeed energy meets real sommelier knowledge.

High share potential: "I'm a Cristom person!"

## Product Idea

Build a playful, highly shareable personality quiz that matches a user to a Willamette Valley winery based on their taste, vibe, hosting style, and weekend preferences.

This should feel lightweight and viral on the surface, but underneath it should use real wine knowledge and region personality.

The result should not just be a winery name. It should feel like a fun identity statement.

Examples:
- "You're a Cristom person."
- "You belong at Elk Cove."
- "Your winery match is Stoller."
- "You're made for a bold, site-driven estate like this one."

## Core Promise

The quiz answers:
- Which winery are you?
- Why that winery matches your personality.
- What wine style fits you.
- What your "wine personality" says about you.
- What you should order next.

## Target Experience

The quiz should be:
- Fast.
- Shareable.
- Visually polished.
- Slightly cheeky.
- Smart enough for wine lovers.
- Easy enough for casual users.

## Quiz Structure

Exactly 4 questions.

Each question should have 4 to 6 answer choices.

The choices should map to winery personalities through a scoring system.

After the final question, the user gets:
- Their matching winery.
- A short personality description.
- A wine recommendation.
- A share card.
- A "why this fits you" explanation.

## Quiz Design Goals

The quiz should feel like:
- A BuzzFeed personality quiz.
- A modern wine discovery tool.
- A playful way to learn Willamette Valley winery identities.
- A shareable social artifact.

The result should be something users want to screenshot and send to friends.

## Region Foundation

Use the Willamette Valley as the home region.

This region is known for:
- Cool-climate winegrowing.
- Pinot noir as the signature grape.
- Warm days and cool nights.
- Diverse soils including volcanic and marine sedimentary types.
- Strong expression of site and sub-AVA identity.

This matters because the quiz should not feel generic. The winery matches should reflect actual wine personalities.

## Featured Winery Personalities

Use a small curated set of wineries with distinct archetypes.

### Cristom
Personality: serious, elegant, expressive, terroir-forward, a little romantic, a little intense.

Use cues like:
- Eola-Amity Hills.
- Volcanic soils.
- Whole-cluster energy.
- Native yeast feel.
- Red fruit, spice, structure, and lift.

Best for people who are:
- Thoughtful.
- Detail-oriented.
- Romantic but grounded.
- Classic with a twist.
- Into depth, precision, and structure.

### Elk Cove
Personality: polished, welcoming, scenic, balanced, cool-climate but friendly.

Use cues like:
- Estate-grown Oregon wines.
- Yamhill-Carlton identity.
- Pinot noir and cool-climate whites.
- Fresh, bright, vibrant fruit.
- Approachability with quality.

Best for people who are:
- Social but not loud.
- Adventurous but dependable.
- Outdoorsy.
- Warm, generous, and easy to like.
- Into clean, lifted, food-friendly wines.

### Stoller
Personality: stylish, modern, refined, inclusive, polished, easy to recommend.

Use cues like:
- Dundee Hills.
- Sustainable, contemporary estate vibe.
- Broad appeal.
- Balanced Pinot noir.
- A winery people bring their friends to.

Best for people who are:
- Trend-aware.
- Design-conscious.
- Friendly and polished.
- Reliable in a group setting.
- The person who plans the reservation.

## Optional Extra Results
If you want more than 3 results, add:
- A wild, experimental boutique winery personality.
- A white-wine-forward Chardonnay/Pinot Gris estate.
- A rustic, earthy old-soul Pinot house.

But the first version should stay tight and shareable.
